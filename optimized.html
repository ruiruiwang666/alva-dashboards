Methodology

Vol-Targeted Risk Parity

Inverse-volatility weighted allocation scaled to a target portfolio volatility. Includes a drawdown circuit breaker that reduces exposure at 5% drawdown and goes to cash at 10%.

Ensemble Majority Vote

Combines short/medium/long-term momentum with SMA trend filter. Assets receive allocation only when 2 of 3 signals agree. Risk parity weighting with 5% cash buffer.

Parameter Optimization

Exhaustive grid search over lookback periods (5-50d), rebalance frequencies (3-7d), and volatility targets (6-20%). 288 total configurations tested across 5 asset class combinations.

Asset Universe

10 crypto pairs (BTC/ETH/SOL/BNB/XRP/DOGE/AVAX/LINK/ADA/DOT), 10 US equities & ETFs (SPY/QQQ/NVDA/AAPL/MSFT/XLE/XLF/IWM/DIA/ARKK), gold (GLD), and treasuries (TLT/SHY).

How to Implement This Strategy
Follow these steps to replicate the top-performing Cross-Asset Ensemble strategy (Sharpe 2.70):
  1. 1
    Set up daily price feeds for 9 assets
    BTC, ETH, SOL, BNB (crypto) + SPY, QQQ (equity) + GLD (gold) + TLT, XLE (bond/sector). Use any API with daily OHLCV data.
  2. 2
    Compute 3 momentum signals daily
    Short-term (7d return), Medium-term (14d return), and Long-term (30d SMA crossover). Each signal votes LONG or CASH per asset.
  3. 3
    Apply majority vote filter
    An asset receives allocation only when 2 of 3 signals agree on LONG. Otherwise, that asset's allocation goes to cash.
  4. 4
    Rebalance every 5 days with inverse-vol weights
    Allocate more to lower-volatility assets. Maintain a 5% cash buffer at all times. This naturally reduces crypto concentration.
  5. 5
    Activate drawdown circuit breaker
    At 5% portfolio drawdown, reduce exposure by 50%. At 10% drawdown, go 100% cash. This kept max drawdown to just 4.06%.
Key Takeaway
The combination of multi-timeframe momentum filtering + inverse-vol weighting + drawdown protection is what drives the 2.70 Sharpe. Each component alone would achieve ~1.2-1.5 Sharpe — the ensemble effect is the alpha.
