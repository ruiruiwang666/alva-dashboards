Why Regular Rebalancing Matters

Portfolio drift occurs naturally as different asset classes generate different returns over time. Left unchecked, a portfolio originally designed for moderate risk can gradually shift toward a more aggressive or conservative stance than intended. For example, during a prolonged equity rally, stock allocations grow while bonds shrink, increasing vulnerability to a downturn at the worst possible moment.

A quarterly rebalancing schedule strikes a practical balance between maintaining target allocations and minimizing transaction costs. Research suggests that rebalancing when any single holding drifts beyond 5% from its target captures most of the risk-reduction benefit. This tool calculates the exact trades needed, accounting for share prices and whole-share constraints, so execution takes minutes rather than a manual spreadsheet exercise.
