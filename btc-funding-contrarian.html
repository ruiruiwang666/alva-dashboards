Objective
The BTC Funding Rate Contrarian strategy exploits mean-reversion opportunities in Bitcoin by monitoring the perpetual futures funding rate and long/short ratio. When the crowd is excessively bearish (negative funding, low L/S ratio), the strategy enters a long spot position, betting that sentiment will revert. It exits when bullish sentiment returns or after a 21-day maximum holding period. The approach is designed to capture mispricings driven by herding behavior in the crypto derivatives market.
Signal Generation
The strategy computes a 7-day rolling average of daily BTC perpetual futures funding rates. This smoothed signal filters out single-day noise and captures the underlying sentiment trend. The long/short ratio from Binance futures is used as a confirmation indicator.
Input 7-day simple moving average of daily BTC perpetual funding rates (BINANCE_PERP_BTC_USDT)
Confirm Binance BTC futures long/short account ratio (top traders)
Entry Rule
Condition Go Long 100% when: 7d avgFundingRate < 0% AND Long/Short Ratio < 1.0
A negative average funding rate means short-sellers are paying longs, indicating the market is net-short and over-bearish. A L/S ratio below 1.0 confirms that short positions outnumber longs among top traders. Together, these signals identify a contrarian long opportunity.
Exit Rule
Signal Exit Close position when: 7d avgFundingRate > 0.05% AND Long/Short Ratio > 1.2
Time Exit Close position after 21 calendar days regardless of signal state
The signal exit triggers when sentiment has flipped from bearish to bullish. The 21-day time stop ensures capital is not locked in a position indefinitely if conditions remain ambiguous.
